Your Eating Protocol
Principles driving every food choice this week

🫀 Heart Priority

Emphasize omega-3s (salmon, cod, sardines), soluble fiber (oats, lentils, beans), and olive oil. Limit saturated fat. These directly target your 10 out-of-range cardiovascular biomarkers.

💪 Muscle Preservation

Target 0.7–0.8g protein per lb of goal bodyweight (~185g/day). Spread protein across 4–5 meals. Prioritize leucine-rich foods: eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt, fish.

🔥 Fat Loss Deficit

At 255 lbs + strength training, your TDEE ≈ 2,900–3,100 cal. Eating 2,200 cal creates a ~700 cal deficit for ~1.5 lb/week loss without sacrificing muscle.

☀️ Vitamin D

One of your 2 out-of-range nutrient markers. This plan includes fatty fish 3x/week, eggs daily, and fortified foods. Consider a D3 supplement (2,000–4,000 IU/day).

🕐 Meal Timing

On training days: eat a protein-rich meal 60–90 min before and within 60 min after training. This maximizes muscle protein synthesis and recovery.

🚫 Avoid

Ultra-processed foods, refined seed oils, sugary drinks, white bread. These drive up inflammation markers (hs-CRP) and directly worsen your heart biomarkers.

How This Plan Targets Your Biomarkers
Every food choice maps to a specific lab result

❤️ Heart (10/15 out of range)

Omega-3 rich fish 3x/week (salmon, cod, sardines) + olive oil as primary fat directly lowers LDL and triglycerides. Walnuts, flaxseed, and chia add additional omega-3s. Soluble fiber from oats, lentils, and beans binds cholesterol. Avoid trans fats and refined carbs which worsen every cardiovascular marker.

🌞 Nutrients (2/20 out of range — likely Vitamin D)

Fatty fish, eggs (daily), and fortified almond milk provide dietary D3. However, food alone rarely fixes low Vitamin D — discuss a D3+K2 supplement (2,000–5,000 IU/day) with your doctor based on your specific level.

🔥 Inflammation (hs-CRP)

Turmeric (curry dish), ginger, dark berries, olive oil, fatty fish, and green leafy vegetables all have clinical evidence for lowering hs-CRP. Eliminating sugar and processed oils is equally important.

💪 Muscle Building at 48

At 48, testosterone and anabolic signaling decline. Counter this with: 185g+ protein daily, leucine-rich meals every 3–4 hours, zinc from pumpkin seeds and beef, and magnesium from spinach and nuts. Sleep 7–9 hours — growth hormone peaks during deep sleep.

🧬 Metabolic Health (all in range!)

Your metabolic markers are good — protect them by avoiding the refined carbs and sugar that would raise fasting glucose and insulin. This plan keeps carbs complex and fiber-rich to maintain your healthy metabolic baseline.

🧠 Stress & Aging (in range)

Maintain this by limiting alcohol, managing training recovery, and including adaptogens — the dark berries, olive oil, and leafy greens in this plan support cellular health and reduce oxidative stress markers.

Sunday Prep Guide
2–3 hours of prep = effortless eating all week

🍳 Cook in Batches

Grill 2 lbs chicken breast. Cook 3 cups brown rice. Cook 2 cups quinoa. Hard-boil 8–10 eggs. These become your building blocks for 5 days of lunches.

🥗 Prep Vegetables

Roast a large sheet pan of mixed veg (bell peppers, zucchini, Brussels sprouts). Wash and dry all greens. Slice cucumbers and celery. Store in airtight containers.

🫙 Make Overnight Oats

Prep Thursday's overnight oats on Wednesday night. Mix oats + protein powder + almond milk + chia seeds in a mason jar, refrigerate overnight.

❄️ Storage Tips

Cooked proteins last 4 days in the fridge. Freeze any fish/meat beyond day 4. Keep cut avocado with lime juice to prevent browning. Portion snacks into bags on Sunday.
