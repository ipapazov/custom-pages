Your Eating Protocol
Principles driving every food choice this week

🫀 Heart Priority

Emphasize omega-3s (grilled salmon, grilled tuna steak), soluble fiber (oats, lentils, beans), and olive oil. Limit saturated fat. These directly target your 10 out-of-range cardiovascular biomarkers.

💪 Muscle Preservation

Target 0.7–0.8g protein per lb of goal bodyweight (~185g/day for you). Spread protein across 4–5 meals. Prioritize leucine-rich foods: eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt, fish.

🔥 Fat Loss Deficit

At 255 lbs + strength training, your TDEE ≈ 2,900–3,100 cal. Eating 2,200 cal creates a ~700 cal deficit for ~1.5 lb/week loss without sacrificing muscle.

☀️ Vitamin D

One of your 2 out-of-range nutrient markers. This plan includes grilled fatty fish 3x/week, eggs daily, and fortified foods. Consider a D3 supplement (2,000–4,000 IU/day).

🕐 Meal Timing

On training days: eat a protein-rich meal 60–90 min before and within 60 min after training. This maximizes muscle protein synthesis and recovery.

Sunday Prep Guide
A full sequence to cook the whole week in one focused session — about 3 hours total
1. Before You Start
10 min
  1. Pull out 8–10 storage containers (mix of large for proteins/grains, small for snacks). Label lids with a piece of tape: "Chicken," "Salmon," "Rice," "Veg," "Snacks."
  2. Preheat the oven to 400°F — it'll be used for roasting veg, sweet potatoes, and one fish fillet.
  3. Fill your largest pot with water for grains, and a second medium pot for eggs. Get both heating now so they're ready when you need them.
  4. Set out 2 sheet pans (more if scaling past 4 people) and line with foil or parchment for easy cleanup.
2. Start the Slow Stuff (grains, eggs, beans)
40 min, mostly hands-off
  1. Eggs: Lower 10 eggs into boiling water. Hard-boil most of them for 10 minutes, then ice-bath and peel — these cover Monday, Sunday snacks and breakfasts. Soft-boil 2 per person separately for 6 minutes for Sunday's breakfast.
  2. Brown rice (3 cups dry): Start in a rice cooker or on the stovetop now — it needs ~40 minutes and covers Monday lunch, Friday lunch, and Thursday dinner.
  3. Wild rice (½ cup dry): Start in a separate small pot — needs ~40–45 minutes, used Sunday dinner.
  4. Quinoa (1 cup dry): Rinse and simmer 15 minutes — covers Monday dinner and Saturday lunch. Start this partway through the rice cook time so both finish close together.
  5. Lentils (1.5 cups dry): Simmer in vegetable broth for 25 minutes — used Tuesday dinner. Can run on a back burner alongside the rice.
  6. While these simmer, drain and rinse your canned chickpeas, cannellini beans, and black beans — have them ready in separate bowls. You'll need ~4 cans total across the week.
💡 Stagger start times so the rice, quinoa, and lentils all finish within about 10 minutes of each other — that way you're not babysitting the stove all afternoon. If you're scaling past 4–6 people, cook grains in two batches rather than one oversized pot — it cooks more evenly.
3. Oven Batch: Sweet Potatoes & Roasted Vegetables
30 min, hands-off once loaded
  1. Pierce 3 sweet potatoes with a fork and place directly on the oven rack at 400°F — roast 40–45 minutes total (set a timer, you'll pull these while doing other prep). Used Wednesday lunch and dinner.
  2. On sheet pan 1: toss 6 bell peppers, 2 zucchini, and 2 pints cherry tomatoes with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast 20–25 minutes — used Monday dinner.
  3. On sheet pan 2: toss 1 lb Brussels sprouts with olive oil. Roast 25–30 minutes — used Wednesday dinner.
  4. Separately, steam or roast 1 lb broccoli florets for 8–10 minutes (steaming keeps it crisp for the Thursday stir-fry, where it'll be reheated briefly).
4. Fire Up the Grill (or grill pan)
35–40 min
  1. Pat dry and season 4 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each, per person) with salt, pepper, and a little olive oil. Grill 4–5 minutes per side until just cooked through. These cover Monday dinner, Thursday lunch (cold/flaked over salad), and Friday dinner — grill one or two extra fillets in case portions run short.
  2. Season and grill 1 tuna steak (6–7 oz per person) for about 2 minutes per side — keep it slightly pink in the center, it overcooks fast. This is for Tuesday's lunch salad — slice once cooled.
  3. Season 2.5 lbs chicken breast with salt, pepper, and a little olive oil. Grill in batches, about 6–7 minutes per side, until internal temp hits 165°F. This covers Wednesday lunch, Wednesday dinner (the herb-crusted version can be seasoned differently — see note below), and Sunday's leftover bowl.
  4. Grill or pan-sear 1 lb shrimp on skewers, about 2 minutes per side until pink and opaque — for Saturday lunch. This is quick, do it last so it doesn't sit out.
💡 Reserve a small portion of plain grilled chicken before seasoning the rest with rosemary and garlic for Wednesday's herb-crusted version — that way Sunday's leftover bowl isn't overly herby. At larger serving sizes, grill in relay batches rather than crowding the grill — crowded fillets steam instead of sear.
5. Stovetop Batch: Ground Meats
20 min
  1. Brown 2 lbs ground turkey (93% lean) in a large skillet with garlic and onion, breaking it up as it cooks, about 8–10 minutes. Split into two batches mentally: one plain for Monday lunch, one seasoned with cumin and lime for Friday's taco bowl.
  2. In the same pan (no need to wash), sear 0.75 lb grass-fed beef strips for Sunday's stir-fry — about 4–5 minutes until browned. Set aside; you'll reheat with the bok choy and mushrooms fresh on Sunday for best texture.
6. Quick Items: Tofu & Fresh Veg Wash
15 min
  1. Press 1 block of tofu now (wrap in a towel, weight with a pan) so it's ready to pan-fry fresh on Thursday — pressing ahead saves time mid-week.
  2. Wash and dry all leafy greens (spinach, arugula, romaine) and store with a paper towel in the container to keep them crisp through the week.
  3. Slice cucumber, celery, and bell peppers for snacks; portion into small containers or zip bags.
  4. Cut 3 avocados in half — wrap unused halves tightly with the pit left in and a squeeze of lime juice to slow browning. Realistically, buy avocados in smaller batches across the week if you can — they ripen better fresh.
7. Portion & Pack
20 min
  1. Portion the grilled chicken into 6 oz servings per person, across 3–4 containers.
  2. Portion most of the grilled salmon fillets into individual containers (keep one set aside for Monday's fresh dinner, no need to portion that one yet).
  3. Divide the cooked rice, quinoa, and wild rice into separate containers — don't mix grains together, since they're used in different meals.
  4. Pack snack bags: nuts into 1 oz portions per person, cut veggies with hummus cups, hard-boiled eggs in their own container.
  5. Label everything with the day it's intended for, written on tape — future-you will thank present-you.

How This Plan Targets Your Biomarkers
Every food choice maps to a specific lab result

❤️ Heart (10/15 out of range)

Grilled salmon and tuna 3x/week + olive oil as primary fat directly lowers LDL and triglycerides. Walnuts, flaxseed, and chia add additional omega-3s. Soluble fiber from oats, lentils, and beans binds cholesterol. Avoid trans fats and refined carbs which worsen every cardiovascular marker.

🌞 Nutrients (2/20 out of range — likely Vitamin D)

Grilled fatty fish, eggs (daily), and fortified almond milk provide dietary D3. However, food alone rarely fixes low Vitamin D — discuss a D3+K2 supplement (2,000–5,000 IU/day) with your doctor based on your specific level.

🔥 Inflammation (hs-CRP)

Turmeric (curry dish), ginger, dark berries, olive oil, fatty fish, and green leafy vegetables all have clinical evidence for lowering hs-CRP. Eliminating sugar and processed oils is equally important.

💪 Muscle Building at 48

At 48, testosterone and anabolic signaling decline. Counter this with: 180g+ protein daily, leucine-rich meals every 3–4 hours, zinc from pumpkin seeds and beef, and magnesium from spinach and nuts. Sleep 7–9 hours — growth hormone peaks during deep sleep.

🧬 Metabolic Health (all in range!)

Your metabolic markers are good — protect them by avoiding the refined carbs and sugar that would raise fasting glucose and insulin. This plan keeps carbs complex and fiber-rich to maintain your healthy metabolic baseline.

🧠 Stress & Aging (in range)

Maintain this by limiting alcohol, managing training recovery, and including adaptogens — the dark berries, olive oil, and leafy greens in this plan support cellular health and reduce oxidative stress markers.
